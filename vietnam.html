Pho

Pho is Vietnam's national dish, a fragrant soup consisting of broth, rice noodles, herbs, and meat, usually beef or chicken. It's a must-try for any visitor to the country.

Banh Mi

Banh Mi is a Vietnamese sandwich that has gained international fame. It combines a crispy baguette with a variety of fillings, including meats, vegetables, and pâté.

Goi Cuon (Spring Rolls)

Goi Cuon, or Vietnamese spring rolls, are a healthy and refreshing appetizer. They are made with rice paper filled with shrimp, pork, fresh vegetables, and herbs, served with a dipping sauce.

Bun Cha

Bun Cha is a Hanoi specialty that consists of grilled pork served over a plate of white rice noodles with herbs and a side of dipping sauce. It's a dish full of flavor and texture.

Cao Lau

Cao Lau is a regional noodle dish from Hoi An. It features chewy noodles, tender slices of pork, fresh herbs, and crispy rice crackers, all served in a savory broth.

Com Tam

Com Tam, or broken rice, is a popular dish in southern Vietnam. It typically includes grilled pork, fried egg, and a variety of accompaniments, all served over a bed of broken rice.

Banh Xeo

Banh Xeo is a crispy, savory pancake made with rice flour, turmeric, and coconut milk, filled with shrimp, pork, and bean sprouts. It's often served with fresh lettuce and herbs.

Cha Ca

Cha Ca is a Hanoi specialty that features fish marinated in turmeric and dill, then grilled and served with vermicelli noodles, peanuts, and fresh herbs.
